# Question - Platform addendum tenant fees (Victoria)

**Status:** Lawyer brief - not legal advice.

Quni appends a Platform & House Rules Addendum to the prescribed VIC Form 1 (Tier 2). Quni is a third-party technology platform; renters otherwise pay zero. The addendum's fee schedule currently charges the renter two fixed fees: (a) a late-checkout fee where the renter vacates after the agreed time, and (b) an international bank transfer administration fee applied to a bond refund.

Consumer Affairs Victoria's prohibited-terms guidance states a renter cannot be required to pay penalties for breaching agreement rules, cannot be required to pay a fixed fee for terminating early unless the basis for calculating that fee is set out in the agreement, and cannot be required to use a third-party service provider nominated by the rental provider. From 31 March 2026, a ban on third-party businesses demanding or receiving prohibited fees applies, and renters have the right to pay rent without additional charges.

Please advise:

1. Is the late-checkout fee a prohibited penalty under the *Residential Tenancies Act 1997* (Vic) and CAV's prohibited-terms list? Is it void if included?
2. Does the "fixed fee with stated calculation basis" exception apply to either fee, and if so what wording is required to make them enforceable?
3. As a third-party platform, does Quni breach the 31 March 2026 third-party-fee provisions or the renter's right to pay rent without additional charges by (a) charging the international bank-transfer administration fee, or (b) passing through a card surcharge on the optional Quni-platform rent-payment method?
4. Does including a prohibited term expose the rental provider - or Quni - to penalties?
5. Which fees, if any, may remain in a Victorian addendum, and how must each be worded?

**Note:** On the Quni Listing tier the landlord manages bond directly and Quni does not handle bond refunds - please advise whether a bond-refund administration fee is even applicable in that arrangement.
